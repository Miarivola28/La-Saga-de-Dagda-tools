The Origin: Alfred Nobel's Story

The Nobel Prize was born from a remarkable act of personal reflection. This vertical timeline highlights the critical moments that transformed Alfred Nobel's legacy from an inventor of explosives to the world's greatest patron of human progress.

1867

Invention of Dynamite

Alfred Nobel, a brilliant chemist and engineer with over 350 patents, invents dynamite, intending it for construction and mining.

1888

"The Merchant of Death"

A French newspaper mistakenly publishes Nobel's obituary, titling it "The merchant of death is dead." This shocks him into reconsidering his legacy.

1895

The Will

Nobel signs his final will, dedicating 94% of his vast fortune to create prizes for those who "have conferred the greatest benefit to humankind."

1901

The First Prizes

Five years after his death, the first Nobel Prizes are awarded, beginning a legacy that continues to inspire the world over a century later.
